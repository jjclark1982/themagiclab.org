Jonathan Vitale

Robot Representations of Emotions through Social Signals

Jonathan Vitale is a PhD candidate at University of Technology Sydney. He graduated in 2010 from University of Milan with a Bachelor degree in Digital Communication and in 2012 from University of Milan - Bicocca with a Master degree in Computer Science. He is currently holding a UTS Presidents Scholarship and collaborating at the MagicLab since July 2013. His current investigation is the representation of emotions in social robots through social signals and models of artificial empathy. Other interests are: Human-Robot social interactions, developmental robotics, affective computing, cognitive sciences/psychology/neuroscience of emotions, biologically inspired models for robots intelligence, dimensionality reduction and manifolds learning, roboethics.

Robot Empathy and Artificial Pain

He receives a Bachelor Degree in Control Systems and Instrumentation from the University of Hasanuddin- UNHAS in 2003, which is one out of ten best universities in Indonesia. Current research interest is Artificial Pain, Self-Awareness Framework for Robot and Robot Empathy. Upon completion, he will continue his work as a lecturer at UNHAS Indonesia
